# Beyond Sunscreen: Everyday Habits That Protect Your Skin

Your [skin](https://cancer.ca/en/cancer-information/cancer-types/melanoma-skin/what-is-melanoma/the-skin) does a lot more than we often realize. It protects your body every day from heat, cold, sunlight, injury, and infection. While sunscreen is important, healthy skin protection goes beyond applying Sun Protection Factor (SPF) before going outside. Small daily habits can make a real difference in protecting your skin and lowering the risk of long-term damage.

Too much exposure to ultraviolet (UV) rays from the sun or tanning beds can increase the risk of [melanoma skin cancer](https://cancer.ca/en/cancer-information/cancer-types/melanoma-skin/what-is-melanoma), one of the most serious forms of skin cancer. In Canada, an estimated [10,800 people](https://cancer.ca/en/cancer-information/cancer-types/melanoma-skin/statistics) were diagnosed with melanoma skin cancer in 2025, and about [1,250 people](https://cancer.ca/en/cancer-information/cancer-types/melanoma-skin/statistics) died from it. The good news is that many cases can be prevented, and early detection can save lives.

Here are 6 simple ways to protect your skin every day while going beyond sunscreen.

## 1. Wear Protective Clothing

Your clothes can help protect your skin from harmful UV rays. Lightweight long sleeves, sunglasses, hats, and tightly woven fabrics can help reduce direct sun exposure, especially during hot summer days.

## 2. Avoid Peak Sun Hours

The sun is strongest between 10 a.m. and 4 p.m. If possible, plan outdoor activities earlier or later in the day. Choosing shade whenever you can also helps protect your skin over time.

## 3. Protect Your Skin Every Day — Even Indoors and on Cloudy Days

UV rays can still reach your skin on cloudy days or even indoors near windows. Making skin protection part of your daily routine can help reduce damage over time.

## 4. Nourish Your Skin With Healthy Foods

Healthy habits can help protect your skin from damage. Drinking water, eating nutritious foods rich in antioxidants, getting enough sleep, and avoiding smoking can all help keep your skin healthier and stronger over time.

## 5. Check Your Skin Regularly

Pay attention to moles or spots that change in size, shape, or color, or areas that itch, bleed, or do not heal. Checking your skin regularly can help you notice [warning signs of melanoma skin cancer](https://cancer.ca/en/cancer-information/cancer-types/melanoma-skin/finding-cancer-early) early, when treatment is often more successful.

## 6. Take Care of Your Immune Health

A weakened immune system can increase the risk of [melanoma](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/melanoma/symptoms-causes/syc-20374884) because the body may not be as effective at finding and removing damaged skin cells caused by UV exposure before they grow into cancer. Some medical conditions and treatments can affect immune health, so regular checkups and skin protection become even more important.

## Small Habits Matter

Protecting your skin does not have to be complicated. Small everyday choices can help lower your risk of skin damage and support healthier skin for years to come. Sometimes, the simplest habits can make the biggest difference.
