# Finding Joy and Purpose After Cancer

*Written by Seyitan Moritiwon*

After a cancer treatment, you hear the words "congratulations, you're now cancer free!" You're elated, of course! But at the back of your mind, you might fear a relapse. That's completely normal and in fact, [studies](https://www.health.harvard.edu/blog/fear-of-cancer-recurrence-mind-body-tools-offer-hope-2019030716152) show that 30% to 70% of cancer survivors report moderate to high levels of fear of cancer recurrence.

Finding joy and purpose after cancer is about learning how to live again after months or even years of appointments, procedures, uncertainty and emotional strain. While the journey is different for every survivor, you might discover something remarkable on the other side of cancer: a renewed appreciation for life, a deeper sense of purpose and unexpected moments of joy.

Here are some tips to find joy and purpose after cancer.

1. **Assess your life:** Pausing, thinking and assessing your life can lead to asking yourself questions about what makes you happy and how you really want to spend your time. You might rediscover old passions or pursue new hobbies.
2. **Practice gratitude:** Gratitude is one of the most powerful lessons cancer survivors carry forward. It does not erase the hardships of cancer, but it can help survivors focus on what remains meaningful and life-giving. You can express gratitude for supportive family members, dedicated healthcare professionals, friendships strengthened through adversity and the opportunity to continue pursuing their goals.
3. **Enjoy nature:** Spending time outdoors can be therapeutic. Taking a walk, sitting in a quiet space outside your home, smelling flowers, and stargazing are ways you can benefit from the outdoors.
4. **Explore a creative activity:** Creative activities including painting, drawing, taking photographs, gardening, and playing a musical instrument might help express emotions and reduce stress.
5. **Give back:** Giving back either through volunteering your time, donating money, starting a campaign to create awareness and fundraising can be a great way to find purpose again. As someone who has gone through the experience, you can support current cancer patients through that lens, and sharing your story can be a meaningful way to give back.

Life after cancer may not look exactly as it did before, but that does not mean it cannot be rich, fulfilling and purposeful.

Building a meaningful life after cancer often involves some of the points listed above as well as setting new goals, nurturing relationships and embracing opportunities for growth. It is about recognizing that survivorship is not just about what was lost, but also about what can still be gained.

The truth is you are a survivor and you beat cancer, and you will surely find purpose in life again.
